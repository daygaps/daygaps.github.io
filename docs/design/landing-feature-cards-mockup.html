What changed, and why

Palette
Page ground moved from the current cool cream (#F7F6F2) to the mockup's warm oatmeal (#E7E1D5). Band sits on --cream #F3EFE7; cards sit on --panel-cream #F1ECE2. Three warm neutrals close enough in value that the eye reads folds in one sheet.
Ink ladder
Four weights instead of black plus one gray: --ink for headings and hard signals, --medium-ink for body copy and quiet fragments, --light-ink for kickers and separators, --whisper-ink for barely-there marks. Every text choice picks the rank it deserves.
Coral discipline
Coral appears in exactly two places on the band: the 2h duration on the "It knows how long" card, and the #stats tag. Everything else is ink, cream, and hairline. The DUE chip stays urgency-red on purpose; that is a separate loud signal for deadlines and lives outside the coral budget.
Type ramp
Kicker: 11 / 700 / uppercase / 1.4px tracked / light-ink. Headline: 34 / 600 / -0.6px / 1.15. Lede: 15 / 1.55 / medium-ink / 54ch. Card head: 15 / 600 / -0.1px. Card body: 13 / 1.5 / medium-ink. Same ramp the app mockup uses.
Card treatment
Panel-cream fill plus hairline border at 9% ink, 8px radius, 14 / 16 padding. No resting shadow. Hover adds a 1px lift and a short 4-10px shadow. That is the whole motion budget for the grid. Matches the decision-box density in the app mockup.
Card-header strip
Each card's viz slot is an edge-to-edge strip on --cream, one shade lighter than the card body, with a hairline underline and the top two corners rounded to match the card. Bootstrap .card-header idiom: same horizontal padding as the body so the fragment inside aligns with the h3 and p below. No inset, no extra depth.
Fragment underline removed
The .frag-gap fragment in the app carries its own bottom hairline for the section break. On the site the card-header strip already provides that separator, so the fragment's line was doubling up. Dropped it. Marketing renderings don't need pixel-for-pixel parity with the app; they need to read well.
Hairlines
One hairline value across the whole system: rgba(31,45,52,0.09). Band top and bottom, card borders, the underline under the gap header, decision-box borders. Half the weight the site uses today (currently 0.18), which was reading as fences instead of folds.
Fragments
Gap header time in ink, name in medium-ink uppercase with 0.4px tracking, hairline underline. Task checkbox is a hairline square in medium-ink. DUE chip stays urgency-red. Area chip moves to cool slate (was mist teal). Tag stays coral-filled and gets the deeper coral for punch on cream.
What isn't ported yet
The other bands on the landing (hero, WHY, four scenes, plain files, CTA, iOS teaser) still run on the current palette. This mockup is the vocabulary; the site-wide port comes next once you approve the vocabulary here.
One thing to look at on the Apple Display. The band's warm-oatmeal ground under the cream cards is where the print-magazine feeling comes from. If it reads instead as "beige and washed out" on your screen, the page-ground value is the first knob to turn (#E7E1D5 in the mockup; darker toward #DBD3C3 gives more contrast against the cards, lighter toward #EEE8DC gives a lighter overall feel).
