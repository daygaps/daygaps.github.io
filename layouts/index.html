{{/* ---------- LONG: deeper about ---------------------------------------- */}} {{/* Expands the medium pitch into a fuller picture. Brings in the two-mode framing explicitly. Ends on the refrain. */}}

What it looks like

A day, in its actual shape.

Some of your day is fixed: meetings, meals, the school run, the walk, the class you teach. Between those points are gaps. Pockets of time the day doesn't claim. They're where reading happens, where the proposal gets written, where the next idea has room to form.

{{ partial "brand-inline.html" . }} is a task manager built around those gaps. You name each one in your own words: morning focus. Afternoon writing. Evening proposal. Each is a small, deliberate intention for a slice of your day.

Two modes follow from that. Before the day starts, you're planning: deciding what each gap is for, what goes in it. Once a gap arrives, you're executing: in it, focused, with everything else faded.

The gap is the unit. That's the whole idea.
