Alongside your calendar

The calendar keeps the events. You get the gaps.

Your calendar is already the system of record for the committed parts of your day, and it stays that way. {{ partial "brand-inline.html" . }} reads the calendars you choose to share and writes nothing back, ever. The events appear alongside your gaps as the fixed posts of the day: a map of where the open time actually is.

Between those posts, the app derives the true gaps and keeps them current. Click one in the calendar pane and it becomes a named gap, starting right where the open time starts. Schedule a task somewhere that turns out to sit inside an event, a meeting or an hour you blocked for something else, and a quiet tint says so.

And because the room in every gap is derived rather than stored, a meeting that runs long leaves nothing to repair. The day moves; the math moves with it.

{{/* ---------- SCENE 4: quiet by design ----------------------------------- */}}

Quiet by design

Every pixel earns its place.

The palette is muted, warm, and calm, and it stays that way so that when color does speak, it means something. Red is a deadline arriving. Coral is the gap you're working in right now. Everything else keeps its voice down.

That restraint extends to how the app treats your plans. Tasks you didn't get to ride forward to the top of Today as Carried over, in the same calm voice as everything else: they're yesterday's plan meeting today's, never a verdict. Real deadlines live in a separate register, and when one needs you, it is allowed to interrupt. The difference between "didn't get to it" and "must be done" is built into the pixels.

Completed work fades to muted strikethrough instead of disappearing. When you focus a gap, the rest of the day eases back. The header is a small pill mark that asks nothing of you.

These are not absences. They are decisions. Every choice the app makes is pointed at the same thing: keeping you in the work and out of the app. {{ partial "brand-inline.html" . }} gets quieter the more you use it.

And on your phone

The same gaps follow you off the desk.

The iPhone app reads the same iCloud zone as the Mac, so the day in your pocket is the day on your desk. Open it between meetings or on the walk to lunch. The Today view is right there, the inbox is one tap away, and the gestures match the way the day actually changes.

Tap to mark a task done. One swipe sets a due date. The other opens reschedule, where a new day and a new gap are one motion. The vocabulary is the same in Today, in This Week, and inside a project. See the phone in more detail.

{{/* ---------- SHARING ----------------------------------------------------- */}} {{/* Shared projects: the plan is common ground, the schedule is personal. */}} {{/* Self-contained band; if sharing slips out of the v1.0 build, this */}} {{/* section and the matching /concepts/ section lift out cleanly. */}}

Shared plans, personal days

Share the project. Keep your schedule.

A project can be shared with other people through iCloud. Everyone you invite sees the same plan: the sections, the tasks and their order, what's done, the deadlines. When each task happens stays personal. You schedule your part into your own gaps, they schedule theirs into theirs, and the plan agrees while the days differ.

One thing this unlocks: put a gap inside the weekly meeting itself and schedule the points you want to raise. Your agenda assembles itself from the shared plan, on your phone, in the room. More in Concepts.

Private by architecture

Your data lives in your iCloud. Nowhere else.

Your areas, projects, and days sync between your devices through your own private iCloud zone. There are no {{ partial "brand-inline.html" . }} servers, no account to create, and no analytics watching you work. The privacy page is short because there is nothing to disclose.

And when you want your plans as text, the bridge writes them to a folder as plain YAML: readable in any editor, diffable in git, and ready to hand to the AI assistant of your choice. The app is meant to be operated with an AI, on your terms.
